Projects I've Worked On

Classifying General Work Activities for ORS Data

Summer 2019 I worked as a Data Science Fellow at the Bureau of Labor Statistics under the Department of Labor in Washington, D.C. My project was a multiclass, multilabel NLP classification task in which I used text data from the Occupational Information Network (O*NET) to broadly classify work activities from the Occupational Requirements Survey (ORS). The purpose of this work is to eventually gain insight into what kinds of jobs are at risk of being automated or moved overseas. For this project I used a variety of Python libraries including Pandas, Numpy, SK-Learn, NLTK, SpaCy. I also played around with some deep learning techniques using Keras with a TensorFlow backend. The code for this project is unavailable as the data is proprietary, however a brief PowerPoint summarizing the project can be found here.

Microbiome Metadata Normalization

In 2017, IBM and UC San Diego's Center for Microbiome Innovation (CMI) teamed up to form the Artificial Intelligence for Healthy Living Center which focuses on conducting groundbreaking research in aging and the human microbiome. Last year, I started working with novel data sets in CMI, normalizing microbiome metadata with Python Pandas in Jupyter Notebooks. Now, I'm working with two teams at CMI to create QIIME 2 plugins to download EBI studies and normalize study metadata. My favorite part of this research is being able gain hands-on experience working with real, messy data in a specialized field like microbiology. I have great mentors and co-researchers at CMI who encourage me to exercise new skills in developing open-source packages and writing optimized code. Both the EBI downloader and the normalization plugin projects are currently in early development stages; git repos are avilable here and here.

Chinese Censorship Research

After attending a talk Dr. Margaret Roberts gave about censorship in China last year, I was super excited to reach out to her and learn more about her research. I started working for Professor Roberts with my classmate, Emily Zhao, using different NLP techniques to parse and analyze chinese legal documents. I'm currently working with post doctoral fellow Young Yang to grab congress members' tweets with the Twitter API, and measure their sentiment towards China based on different events like the Chinese-American trade war or the Hong Kong protests. My favorite part about working with Dr. Roberts is always getting to work on something different, exercising skills in various aspects of data science, and knowing that what we're studying is relevent to actual events happening in real time.

Optimizing Nutrition Under Budgetary Constraints

In 2018, a team of my data science classmates and I were awarded Haligioglu Data Science Institute fellowships to pursue data science-related projects. We initially tried to address the issue of food insecurity in underserved communities, and the project we ended up with was a webapp that used linear optimization to produce nutritionally-optimal grocery lists with user-specified budget constraints. At the end of our fellowship term, we determined we needed to understand which macronutrients and micronutrients should be prioritized in an individual's diet, which is ultimately a nutrition science problem. The final product was not able to provided grocery lists that were as specialized as we initially intended, but each of us learned a lot about the process of seeing a data science project from the early stages of brainstorming to a completed product.
