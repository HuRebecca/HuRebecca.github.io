How I Became a Data Scientist


I came into college studying human biology, but pipetting E. Coli onto agar plates just wasn't my thing! However, from my brief stint as a bio major, I discovered my love for Excel spreadsheets. I decided to take a chance on UCSD's newest major, Data Science and four years later here I am.

Data Science at UC San Diego


Being part of the first graduating class of UC San Diego's Data Science Program has been both an honor and an adventure. Don't get me wrong, being in the guinea pig class for any new major certainly comes with a few bumps in the road (like having to take classes at 9 PM, or not knowing if I'd be able to graduate on time), but I would never consider another major. I've enjoyed and learned something new in every data science class I've taken here.

Each of the lower divison courses introduced me to a new aspect of data science that I could directly see myself using in the future. Each of the upper divison courses I've taken so far have tested my ability to apply the skills I've previously learned and pushed me to ask critical questions about my own understanding of communicating the truth. I've been taught by the most interesting, helpful, and passionate professors in my data science courses, and advised by the hardest working program coordinators on campus.

As I come to the end of my time at UC San Diego, I'm looking back at the countless late nights and all nighters I've spent completing PAs and math homework assignments fondly. I feel ready and excited to begin my career as a data scientist and I hope that if you're considering majoring or minoring in data science at UC San Diego, you'll approach the coursework ethusiasm and tenacity, and engage in the amazing community here!

My Interests


One of the reasons I felt data science was such a perfect fit for me was its ability to be applied in virtually every field. I declared a minor in political science after taking POLI 10: American Politics and POLI 13: International Relations, because I discovered just how curious our policy-making system is, and how precariously our relationships with other nations are formed. I am developing my interest in the intersection of political science and data science with an undergraduate researcher position in the School of Global Policy and Strategy, studying Chinese censorship.

Beyond data science applications in the social sciences, I've also worked in other fields like microbiology and nutrition. You can check out other projects I've worked on by clicking the tab above. As someone who goes on way too many Google/Wikipedia deep dives, there's so many topics and so much data I look forward to exploring someday (including the stock market, differential privacy, and the potential of bamboo to sequester C02 out of the atmosphere). With each of these new projects I love diving into the domain knowledge and figuring out how to integrate the unique attributes of the what I'm studying with my abilities in cleaning, EDA, analysis, ML, and visualization.

Another one of my major interests in data science is the ethical dilemma that come with deploying models and algorithms that can potentially discriminate against disenfranchised groups and negatively influence peoples' lives. Its of the upmost importance to consider how the models we create treat outliers, and people who fall outside of what society considers "normal". We need to recognize the biases we hold when we create our models and take the necessary steps to expose biases we might be blind to. I think discussing the implicit biases we hold and the consequences these biases can have on equality is part of a greater conversation our society needs to have on equality and discrimination. If you're interested in discussing ethics in data science with me, feel free to reach out to me through email!
